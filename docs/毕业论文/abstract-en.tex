\chapter*{ABSTRACT}

With the rapid development of generative artificial intelligence, AI-generated content has been widely applied to image synthesis, video generation, digital human animation, and intelligent media creation. However, the increasing realism and accessibility of these technologies have also introduced serious security challenges, including forged content dissemination, unsafe media generation, identity misuse, and growing pressure on content moderation. Focusing on the security risks of AI-generated content in practical scenarios, this thesis studies fake content detection and unsafe content recognition for images and videos, and further designs a multimodal content security detection and analysis platform.

This thesis first reviews the development of AIGC generation technologies, deepfake-related manipulation methods, and existing security detection approaches, and analyzes their differences in detection targets, generalization ability, deployment cost, and engineering adaptability. On this basis, a three-layer system architecture is designed, consisting of a front-end presentation layer, a back-end proxy layer, and a model inference layer. By integrating locally deployed detection models with external multimodal review APIs, the platform forms a unified workflow covering content input, security analysis, risk grading, and visualized result presentation. The system supports image and video upload, URL-based input, fake content identification, unsafe content review, result visualization, and output management, which provides an effective foundation for later experiments, sample construction, and application verification.

Beyond the current prototype implementation, this thesis also extends the design from the perspectives of multi-model collaboration, comparative evaluation of detection methods, unified risk scoring, and experimental assessment. Therefore, the work not only reflects the engineering practice of building a deployable tool, but also strengthens the research depth required for an undergraduate thesis in the field of AIGC security.

\vspace{1em}
\noindent\textbf{Key words:} AIGC security, multimodal detection, fake content detection, unsafe content recognition, content security platform
