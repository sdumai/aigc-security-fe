\vspace{1.5\baselineskip}

With the rapid development of generative artificial intelligence, AI-generated content has been widely used in image synthesis, video generation, digital human animation, and intelligent media creation. Its increasing realism and accessibility also bring security risks such as forged content dissemination, unsafe media generation, identity misuse, and moderation pressure. Focusing on practical AIGC security scenarios, this thesis studies fake content detection and unsafe content recognition for images and videos, and designs a multimodal content security detection and analysis platform.

This thesis reviews AIGC generation technologies, deepfake manipulation methods, and mainstream detection approaches, comparing their detection targets, generalization ability, deployment cost, and engineering adaptability. Based on this analysis, a three-layer architecture is proposed, including the front-end presentation layer, back-end proxy layer, and model inference layer. By combining locally deployed detectors with external multimodal review APIs, the platform builds a unified workflow for content input, security analysis, risk grading, and result visualization. It supports image and video upload, URL-based input, fake content identification, unsafe content review, and output management.

In the experimental analysis, image fake detection, video content analysis, unsafe content review, and platform usability are verified according to the current implementation. The local image detector is evaluated on a ProGAN mini subset as a reproducible baseline, and the back-end proxy converts local model outputs and external API responses into structured results for front-end visualization. The results show that the platform has formed a basic closed loop from content generation and detection to risk presentation, providing a practical reference for deployable AIGC content governance tools.
